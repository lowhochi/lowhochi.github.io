My Vocabulary Page

compare to, compare with  

compare to: use compare to when highlighting the similarities of one thing to another

compare with: use compare with when you are looking for differences

1/ He compared this year's historic win to that of 1992 Dream Team.

2/ They won the championship in a clean sweep, compared with last year's
    game 7 loss.



albeit   conj   //

(meaning) although, even though

albeit is never used to introduce an independent clause

1/ The evening were very pleasant, albeit a little quiet.

2/ an enjoyable, albeit expensive, vacation



alibi   n(c)   //

不在犯罪現場的證據

1/ He has a cast-iron(=very strong) alibi -
    he was in hospital the week of the murder

an excuse for something bad or a failure

2/ After eight years in power, the government can no longer use
    the previous government's policy as an alibi for its own failure.



hold up   //

to remain strong or successful

1/ Will his alibi hold up in court?

2/ I hope the repairs hold up until we can get to a garage.



resilient   adj   //

able to quickly return to a previous good condition

1/ This rubber ball is very resilient and immediately springs back into shape.

2/ She's a resilient girl - she won't be unhappy for long.



asthma   n(u)   //

(US) /'æzmə/

1/ an asthma sufferer

2/ an asthma attack



rant   v(i)   //

to speak or shout in a loud or angry way

1/ I got really wound up and started ranting and raving.

2/ He's always ranting about the government.

3/ I got fed up with my mother ranting and raving about my clothes all the time.



chrysanth   n(c)   //

a short form of chrysanthemum //



at your service  

saying that you are willing to help them in any way you can

1/ I'm totally at your service - just tell me what you need me to do.



rocky   adj   //

synonym:   rough, uneven

made of rock and therefore usually rough and difficult to travel along

1/ She scrambled along the rocky path.

uncertain and difficult and not likely to last long (unstable)

2/ Their relationship got off to a rocky start.



rough   adj   //

not even or smooth, often because of being in bad condition

1/ It was a rough mountain road, full of stones and huge holes.

not exact or detailed

2/ She made a rough estimate/calculation of the likely cost.

not made in a careful or expensive way

3/ I made a rough table out of some old boxes.

difficult or unpleasant

4/ It must be rough to have two kids and nowhere to live



push back  

to delay something so that it happens later than planned; postpone

1/ The target date for construction has been pushed back.

2/ push back the deadline for the homework



strip   v(t)   //

strip somebody of something: to take away something important from
    someone as a punishment, for example their title, property, or power

1/ Captain Evans was found guilty and stripped of his rank.

2/ He was attempting to win back the title that was stripped from him
    in 1967 for refusing to fight in the Vietnam War.

to take out everything that is not part of the structure of a room or building

3/ The couple first stripped the kitchen of its all units and appliances.


strip off: to remove your clothing, or to remove the clothing of someone else

4/ Suddenly he stripped off and ran into the sea.

5/ He had been striped (off) naked, beaten and robbed.

6/ I stripped the blankets off the bed.

7/ It was so hot that we stripped off our shirts.



deprive ... of ...   v(t)   //

to take something, especially something necessary or pleasant, away from someone

1/ He claimed that he had been deprived of his freedom/rights.

2/ You can't function properly if you are deprived of sleep.



setback   n(c)   //

something that happens that delays or prevents a process from developing

1/ Sally had been recovering well from her operation, but yesterday she experienced/suffered a setback.

2/ There has been a slight/temporary setback in our plans.
