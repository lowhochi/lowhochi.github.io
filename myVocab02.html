My Vocabulary Page

compare to, compare with  

compare to: use compare to when highlighting the similarities of one thing to another

compare with: use compare with when you are looking for differences

1/ He compared this year's historic win to that of 1992 Dream Team.

2/ They won the championship in a clean sweep, compared with last year's
    game 7 loss.



albeit   conj   //

(meaning) although, even though

albeit is never used to introduce an independent clause

1/ The evening were very pleasant, albeit a little quiet.

2/ an enjoyable, albeit expensive, vacation



alibi   n(c)   //

不在犯罪現場的證據

1/ He has a cast-iron(=very strong) alibi -
    he was in hospital the week of the murder

an excuse for something bad or a failure

2/ After eight years in power, the government can no longer use
    the previous government's policy as an alibi for its own failure.



hold up   //

go past

You go past something when you just see it as you're walking or driving by

1/ I went past the store the other day but it was closed.



pass through

You pass through something if you go through some part of it as
  you're walking or driving.

1/ I passed through the tunnel on my way here.

2/ To get to many places from Narita by train you must pass through Tokyo.

scenery   n(u)   //

the general appearance of the natural environment, especially when it is beautiful

1/ beautiful/breathtaking/spectacular scenery

2/ They stopped at the top of the hill to admire the scenery.


scenery is everything that makes up the scene you are looking at. It can be entirely
  manmade structures--buildings, streets, etc. A landscape is a view that includes mainly
  the land and its features--hills and mountains, trees and other plants, streams and
  ponds, etc. A seascape is an ocean scene. A cityscape is an urban scene.
  The scenery can be a landscape, a seascape, a cityscape, or some combination.
