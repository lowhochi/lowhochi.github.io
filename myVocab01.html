pander to someone/something   //

to please other people by doing or saying something
what you think they want to do or say

1/ It's not good the way she panders to his every whim /wɪm/.

2/ She accused the other candidate of pandering to radical environmental groups.



flatter   v(t)   //

to praise someone in order to make them feel attractive or important,
    sometimes in a way that is not sincere

1/ I knew he was only flattering me because he wanted to borrow some money.


flatter yourself: to believe something good about yourself although it might not be true

2/ Steve flatters himself that he is an excellent speaker.


be/feel flattered: to feel very pleased and proud because someone has said
    good things about you or has made you feel important

3/ They were flattered to be invited to dinner by the mayor /mer/.

4/ She was flattered by his attention.


to make someone look more attractive than usual

5/ Short skirts don't flatter me at all.

driving force   (+ for/behind/in something)

behind consumer spending.

2/ Trade is the driving force for sustained economic prosperity.

3/ Women are definitely a driving force in the industry, she said.



few chances vs little chance

little chance: the probability of something occuring is low

few chances: the number of opportunities of doing something are rare.
    For example, if the project can only work during a solar eclipse,
    it would have few chances of success in any given year.

1/ The project has little chance of success. (It will probably not work)

2/ I have little chance of swimming one hundred meters in 105 seconds.

3/ I have few chances to go to comedies because my wife does not like them.
(but when the opportunity does arise, I will very likely succeed in seeing the movie)



not only ... but also ...

1/ Not only was it raining all day at the wedding but also the band was late.

2/ Not only did she forget my birthday, but she also didn't even apologise for forgetting it.

3/ The car not only is economical but also feels good to drive.

4/ The war caused not only destruction and death but also generations of hatred between two communities.

in principle

keep something at bay

to control something and prevent it from causing you problems

2/ The doctors have been able to keep her illness at bay for several months.



on sale, for sale

for sale: If you're selling something, it is for sale;
    offered to be sold, made available to purchasers /'pɜrtʃəsɚ/.

on sale: If you lower the price, it goes on sale;
    able to be bought at reduced prices.

1/ Student A: "I need to buy a widget."
Student B: "Go to Jumbo. They have them for sale."

2/ Student A: "I bought my new skis /sgi:z/ for 900 Swiss Francs."



on demand

synonym:   upon request, on request

1/ They believe that abortion should be available on demand.

beyond vs behind    

distance: behind is the more general word. Beyond has the feeling of long distance

1/ behind your back

2/ behind the house

3/ The town is beyond the mountain.


behind

4/ They'd been forced to leave behind their businesses and possessions.

5/ The bus was behind schedule.

6/ Now that the divorce is behind us, we can move on. (= the divorce is finished)

7/ the man behind the modernization of the organization

8/ The country was behind the president.


beyond

9/ Few children remain in school beyond the age of 16.

10/ The situation has changed beyond recognition.

11/ How he managed to find us is beyond me. (= I cannot understand it)

12/ Her commitment to her profession is beyond doubt. (= no one can doubt it)

13/ The mechanic announced that the engine was beyond repair.


examples from VOA English

14/ Mail deliveries usually fall behind around the winter holidays,
  but they improve beyond the new year.

15/ People need to know math through their school years and beyond.

16/ During the pandemic, many people got behind on their rent.
  (= the rent payments were arriving late)

17/ (get behind with) She got behind with her mortgage,
  and the house was repossessed.

surpass   v(t)   //

to do or be better than

2/ Our team's achievements surpass those of teams in earlier years.
