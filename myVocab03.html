My Vocabulary Page

hold back

hold back something: to stop something from happening or advancing,
  or to keep someone from doing something

1/ She held back from interfering in their arguments.

2/ Sandbags will hold back the flood waters for a while

hold someone/something back: to stop someone/something developing
  or doing as well as they should

3/ She felt that having children would hold her back.

4/ She hadn't got the promotion she wanted, and she accused her boss of
  holding her back.

hold back on something: to wait for a period of time before doing something

5/ We held back on the product release until the third quarter.
